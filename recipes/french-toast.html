French Toast Recipe

Description

This recipe is taken from Allrecipe

Who doesn't like French Toast? It's fast and easy to make, perfect if you're on a rush! According to Wikipedia:
French toast is a dish of sliced bread soaked in beaten eggs and often milk or cream, then pan fried.
Alternative names and variants include "eggy bread", "Bombay toast", "gypsy toast", and "poor knights".

The best breads for French toast are brioche, sourdough, French bread, or challah. These varieties are dense and sturdy enough
to handle total saturation in the wet, milky, egg custard without falling apart. However, in a pinch, any thick-sliced white bread will do.

What you need:

  • White Bread
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Vanilla and cinnamon
  • Salt
  • Butter

Main Ingredients:

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extrsct (Optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • salt
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Directions

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat.
  4. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  5. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  6. and done, serve hot!
